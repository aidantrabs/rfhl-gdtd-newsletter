Operational Reality

Internal Pain: The Hidden Cost of Staying the Same

What isn't visible to customers is eroding the bank's efficiency, compliance posture, and competitive capacity from the inside.

🔗
No Unified View of the Customer & Risk
Phoenix, ECM (NewGen), Euronet, and manual spreadsheets operate in silos. A customer delinquent in Trinidad can apply for a new mortgage in Guyana without detection. No group-level risk, concentration, or portfolio analytics exist.
RISK: Unlimited
Cross-territory credit exposure uncontrolled
📋
Manual Credit Scoring Rule-Based and Siloed
Credit risk scoring performed manually via rule-based spreadsheets, per territory. No enterprise-level predictive analytics. Conservative decision-making limits growth while exposing the bank to underwriting risk through lack of forward-looking signals.
COST: Growth Lost
Overly conservative decisions suppress origination volume
📬
CCU Labor Cost TTD $6 Million Annually
42+ CCU staff primarily spend their time on manually drafting arrears letters, physically folding and stuffing envelopes, copying and pasting email addresses, entering manual NPL relegation entries in Phoenix, and reconciling DCA commissions via spreadsheets.
TTD ~$6M/yr
42 FTEs — ~7 FTEs directly saveable via automation
⚖️
Compliance & AML Risk Heightened by Manual Processes
Manual AML-4A/5/11 form completion, manual KYC document collection, and manual credit bureau screenshots as evidence create significant compliance risk. As regulatory scrutiny increases across Caribbean jurisdictions, the bank's exposure grows.
RISK: Regulatory
Central Bank NPL guidelines breachable without automation
🚀
Time-to-Market Months per Product & Territory
Without a unified platform, each new offering like green mortgages or BNPL must be created from scratch in every territory, requiring unique configurations and compliance approvals. In contrast, competitors with a single platform can launch once and easily replicate across markets.
COST: Speed Gap
RFHL rebuilds every time; competitors iterate once
📉
Missed Revenue — Demand Absorbed or Lost to Competitors
Guyana’s 25–40% mortgage demand surge is either absorbed by branch queues or captured by digital-first competitors. In BVI, the RBI-driven luxury real estate boom generates only 7 mortgages per month under manual processing. Caribbean fintech volume is growing 46%+ annually.
USD $4.5M+
